All living forms are organized in a nested hierarchy. Genes and other replicating nucleic acids formed genomes; Eukaryotic cells were formed by the union of free-living eubac- teria and proto-eukaryotes. In all cases, previously autonomous entities came together to form new wholes higher in the biological ladder. This dynamic process is a transition in individuality. The objective of this report is to assess the evolutionary individuality of bacterial plasmids, autonomous replicating DNA entities enclosed within bacterial cells.
